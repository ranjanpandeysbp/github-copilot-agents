# The Wizard's Cleaner: An Extended Tale

## Story

Aldric the Great was a powerful wizard whose tower resembled a tornado aftermath. Spell books were scattered everywhere, potion ingredients mixed with laundry, and mysterious objects floated randomly through halls. His chaotic magic matched his chaotic home.

One day, desperate for order, Aldric hired Marge—a no-nonsense cleaner who'd worked in libraries and appeared unimpressed by levitating socks.

Marge's first act was organizing every spell book alphabetically by title. Aldric was horrified but exhausted, so he complied. The next morning, disaster struck.

When he needed to banish demons attacking the village, he instead cast "Accidentally Transform into a Llama" (category A). The demons laughed. Confused, Aldric stumbled through pages trying to find "Banish Evil Spirits" (category B), and transformed into three more llamas in the process.

The enemy kingdom heard of the "Great Llama Wizard" and assumed he was a joke deity. They sent their worst warriors—who fled in panic upon seeing a spitting llama wearing a pointy hat.

Marge continued working, unbothered. Aldric became increasingly frustrated—literally becoming sheep, chickens, and confused woodland creatures mid-battle.

Yet something unexpected happened: his enemies stopped taking him seriously. Kingdoms began sending diplomatic envoys with carrots instead of weapons. Aldric the Accidental Llama became the most respected—and feared—peacetime wizard in history.

He eventually learned to embrace the chaos of alphabetical casting, discovering that unpredictability was sometimes more powerful than perfection.

## Moral

Sometimes the greatest power lies not in perfect control, but in accepting disorder and finding unexpected strength in chaos. The most effective solutions often come from embracing what we initially resist.
